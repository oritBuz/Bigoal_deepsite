Simple 3-Step Testing Process

ELISA-Based Technology

VacciCheck uses advanced ELISA technology to measure antibody levels against core vaccines (Canine Distemper, Adenovirus, Parvovirus for dogs; Panleukopenia for cats).

The test requires just a few drops of serum or plasma, with clear colorimetric results that are easy to interpret.

1

Collect Sample

Draw 0.5ml of blood and separate serum or plasma. Only 3 drops needed for testing.

2

Run Test

Add samples to wells, incubate, wash, and add conjugate. Results develop in 30 minutes.

3

Interpret Results

Compare color intensity to reference card. Results indicate positive, equivocal, or negative immunity.

Frequently Asked Questions

VacciCheck has been validated against gold standard virus neutralization tests with excellent correlation (94-98% agreement). The ELISA method provides reliable results for clinical decision-making while being more practical for in-clinic use than sending samples to external labs.

VacciCheck kits have a shelf life of 18 months when stored at 2-8°C (refrigerated). Once opened, the plates can be used for up to 2 weeks if stored properly with desiccant. The conjugate should be used within 1 week after opening.

We provide client education materials with your order. The key points to communicate: 1) Titer tests measure existing immunity, 2) They help avoid unnecessary vaccinations, 3) They're particularly valuable for pets with vaccine reactions or immune issues, and 4) Many boarding facilities now accept titer results in place of vaccination certificates.

You'll need a centrifuge for serum separation, a 37°C incubator (or heat block), a multichannel pipette (recommended but not required), and a timer. Most clinics already have this basic equipment. We provide detailed protocols and support to help you implement the testing.
